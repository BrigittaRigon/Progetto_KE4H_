DESCRIBE query

The first step was using a DESCRIBE query to see the properties and objects belonging to Porta Castiglione. I found it useful to see if it is already well described or not, and to see which properties may be missing.

It appears that Porta Castiglione is already well-described in the ArCo ontology, but we could definitely enrich its profile with some interesting additional information, such as the ancient name it was known by and the ring of walls it belongs to.

2nd Step: How LLMs can enhance the ArCo knowledge graph

I used three different LLMs, ChatGPT, DeepSeek, and Gemini, to help me find the missing information. I asked each model the same question and then evaluated and compared their answers.

Zero-shot prompt results

Question: ‚ÄúTell me the ancient name Porta Castiglione used to be known as and the ring of walls it belongs to‚Äù

ChatGPT‚Äôs answer: Accurate answers, lacked in-depth detail, included sources and images.

Gemini‚Äôs answer: Correct but brief, no sources cited.

Deep Seek‚Äôs answer: Longest and most accurate. Included detailed historical context:

  • Ancient Name: Porta Saragozza
  • Ring of Walls: Cerchia dei Torresotti (Second Walls), built in the 12th century, begun around 1176
  • Common Names: Gates often under small towers (torresotte)
  • Structure: Not as high as later third walls, incorporated into later buildings
  • Visibility Today: Some remnants still visible, e.g., Porta delle Lame
  • Origins & Name Change: Originally named after Via Saragozza, renamed Porta Castiglione in 16th century after Strada Castiglione
